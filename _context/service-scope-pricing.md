## Tier 2: Reservation / Booking System

John's old site had a basic HTML form that emailed submissions. For a transportation company in 2026, he needs something better. Options:

**Option A — Simple Form (Included in Tier 1)**
Contact/inquiry form that emails John and Bobby. Client fills out name, date, pickup, dropoff, passengers, vehicle preference. No real-time availability. This is basically what he had before.

**Option B — Integrated Booking Widget**
Third-party booking tool (like Limo Anywhere, Ground Widjet, or a custom form with Calendly-style scheduling) embedded on the site. Client can request a booking, see vehicle options, and get confirmation.

**Option C — Full Booking Platform**
Dedicated limo/transportation booking software (Limo Anywhere, Book Rides Online, or LimoAnywhere) with dispatch integration, pricing engine, and client portal. This is what modern limo companies use.

| Option | Family Rate | Market Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| A: Simple inquiry form | Included | Included | What he had before |
| B: Booking widget integration | $300–$500 | $500–$1,500 | Monthly software fee ~$50–$150 |
| C: Full booking platform | $500–$800 setup | $1,000–$3,000 setup | Monthly software fee ~$100–$300 |

**Recommendation:** Start with Option A for launch, discuss Option B as a Phase 2. Option C is overkill unless John wants to modernize dispatch.

## Tier 3: Email Infrastructure (The Ticking Time Bomb)

This is the biggest risk in John's business right now and he probably doesn't realize it. If he's hosting email on a local machine at his house:

- Power outage = no email
- Hard drive failure = all email history gone forever
- No redundancy, no backups, no spam filtering
- Deliverability is probably terrible (self-hosted mail servers get flagged as spam constantly)

**The fix:** Migrate to Google Workspace or Microsoft 365.

| Service | Family Rate | Market Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Workspace setup (domain, MX records, accounts) | $200 | $500–$1,000 | |
| Email migration (existing mail → Google Workspace) | $150 | $300–$800 | Depends on mailbox count/size |
| Staff training (30-min walkthrough) | Included | $100–$200/hr | |
| **Total** | **$350** | **$900–$2,000** | |

**Ongoing cost to John:** Google Workspace Business Starter is $7/user/month. For 2-3 users (John, Bobby, maybe dispatch), that's $14–$21/month. This replaces a fragile home server with enterprise-grade email, calendar, and cloud storage.

**⚠️ NEED CLIENT INPUT:**
- How many email accounts does he need? (john@, bobby@, info@, dispatch@?)
- Does he have years of archived email he wants migrated?
- Is he actually self-hosting or using something like GoDaddy/Yahoo email?
